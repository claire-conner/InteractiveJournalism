Alphabet reaches $4 trillion market value

Google's Mountain View Headquarters.

By Claire Conner

The Google parent company hit a $4 trillion market capitalization Monday, joining Nvidia as one of two companies currently above that value.

Alphabet's shares increased by 65.4% last year, driven by investor confidence in its artificial intelligence progress. This included enthusiasm around its recent launch of Gemini 3, which beat competing AI models in a variety of intelligence categories.

Wall Street Journal graph showing tech companies' changed market value over time.

It became the fourth company to trade at a market capitaliation of over $4 million, with Microsoft and Apple reaching that value last year before dipping to $3.8 and $3.6 trillion, respectively.

The tech giants, along with Amazon, are America's most valuable corporations, each worth over $2.5 trillion:

  • Nvidia: $4.53 trillion
  • Alphabet: $4.00 trillion
  • Apple: $3.85 trillion
  • Microsoft: $3.57 trillion
  • Amazon: $2.65 trillion
